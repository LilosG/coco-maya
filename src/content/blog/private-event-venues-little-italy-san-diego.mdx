Little Italy has enough private dining options packed into a few blocks that the actual question isn't "where can I host this," it's "which configuration of which venue fits what I'm planning." Most restaurants in the neighborhood default to one kind of space, a semi-private room, a banquet hall. Coco Maya runs six distinct configurations off the same building, and picking the right one matters more than picking the venue itself.

## The Six Ways to Book This Space

| Event Type | Guests | Typical Length | Space |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bachelorette Parties | 8 – 120 | 2 – 3 hrs | Patio or Buyout |
| Birthday Celebrations | 10 – 180 | 2 – 4 hrs | Room, Patio, or Venue |
| Rehearsal Dinners | 15 – 60 | 3 hrs | Private Dining Room |
| Corporate Events | 10 – 100 | 2 – 3 hrs | Private Room or Patio |
| Baby Showers | 10 – 40 | 2 – 3 hrs | Reserved Patio |
| Evening Buyouts | 80 – 180 | 4 – 5 hrs | Full Venue |

The numbers matter more than they look like they should. A rehearsal dinner tops out at 60 because that's where the private dining room's toast-friendly layout stops making sense for a bigger group, past that, you're better off in patio seating. A baby shower caps at 40 because that's the daylight patio section, not the whole space. Evening buyouts start at 80 because anything smaller doesn't justify closing the venue to regular service. These aren't arbitrary; they reflect what actually works in each space.

## What You're Actually Choosing Between

**The private dining room.** Up to 60 guests, connected to the main bar without being exposed to it. This is the answer for rehearsal dinners, corporate meetings that need AV, and birthday dinners that want to feel intimate rather than loud.

**The elevated patio.** Up to 120 guests, glass atrium bar, open-air. This is where bachelorette parties, baby showers, and most birthday celebrations land, daylight for daytime bookings, string lights and skyline once the sun goes down for evening ones.

**A full venue buyout.** Up to 180 guests, the entire patio and dining room closed to regular service. This is the move for milestone birthdays, large wedding parties, and company holiday parties, anything where "reserved section" undersells what you're planning.

## What You're Actually Booking Into

Coco Maya runs on a Spanish-style coal-fired Josper oven with a Latin-Caribbean coastal menu, and it's part of Grind & Prosper Hospitality, the group behind Miss B's Coconut Club, Park 101, and Cococabana, so the private events side isn't a bolt-on to a restaurant that's never done this before. As a general planning range, expect roughly $20-$50 per person for food and $12-$20 per cocktail; get exact numbers for your group size when you request a proposal.

## Where the Neighborhood Actually Differs

Little Italy is dense with private dining, most of it built around traditional Italian cuisine in indoor banquet rooms, semi-private spaces in the 15-45 guest range, and restaurant buyouts for larger groups. That's a real option if the classic Italian-restaurant-private-room setting is what you're after.

What's harder to find is a genuinely open-air, elevated setting instead of a repurposed back room. That's the specific gap Coco Maya's patio sits in, and it's the reason the venue ends up on shortlists for events that want a different kind of backdrop than four walls and a projector screen.

## Booking Timeline by Group Size

- **Under 20 guests:** 3 to 4 weeks out is usually enough
- **20-80 guests (partial patio or full room):** 4 to 6 weeks
- **80+ guests or a full buyout:** 6 to 8 weeks, longer around December and peak wedding season

Ask about minimum spend when you inquire, it scales with group size and day of week, and knowing it early avoids finding out after you've built the rest of the plan around a space.
