If you're bringing more than 6 or 8 people to Comic-Con — a studio team, an agency activation, a cosplay crew, a group of friends who fly in every year — a walk-in table isn't going to work during convention week. Most Little Italy and Gaslamp restaurants cap walk-in party sizes on Comic-Con weekend, and the venues that don't cap group size are usually the ones so packed it doesn't matter.

The better move for any group over 10 is booking a private space ahead of time. Here's how that actually works, and why booking now — with Comic-Con less than two weeks out — is the difference between having a plan and scrambling for a table on July 22nd.

## Why Groups Book Private Space During Comic-Con Week

- **Convention week changes restaurant math.** Kitchens and floor staff plan for their normal Thursday-through-Sunday volume. Comic-Con adds a surge on top of that, which means walk-in groups get split up, seated in shifts, or turned away outright once a restaurant hits capacity.
- **A private space means your group actually gets to talk.** Whether it's a corporate team debriefing the day's meetings or a friend group comparing panel notes, a semi-private or fully private space beats trying to hold a conversation across a packed bar.
- **It solves the badge-day logistics problem.** Groups coming straight from the Convention Center don't want to split into smaller tables just because nobody planned ahead. One reserved space for the whole group removes that entirely.
